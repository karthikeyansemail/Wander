What We Do? Why We do?



Not so long ago were the times when ‘Mental Health’ was given only superficial importance and not enough heed to. But since the past two years, it has evolved to become a buzzword and talk of the town. With the onset of pandemic and various lockdowns imposed, more people have fallen prey to deteriorating mental health conditions. It has become so tough that many countries have started observing mental health disorders and depression as critical health conditions. Bereavement, isolation, loss of income, and fear are factors triggering these conditions or exacerbating existing ones. With the pandemic, many people have also developed increased levels of alcohol and drug use, anxiety, and insomnia.

At the same time, COVID-19 alone can lead to neurological and mental complications, such as delirium, agitation, and stroke. People with pre-existing mental, neurological or substance abuse disorders are more vulnerable to SARS-CoV-2 infection; they face a higher risk of suffering severe outcomes and at times, even death.

Problem

A study by Assocham showed that nearly 43 percent of employees in the private sector in India suffer from mental health issues at work. Also, a WHO report in 2017 reveals that 18 percent of global depression cases originate from India.

While mental health issues in the workplace are a reality for most companies, the stigma associated with mental health problems often prevents people from seeking help. To tackle this illness, one of the best and proven solutions is to travel and explore the world.

Did you know?

According to a Cornell University study, the anticipation of a trip can increase your happiness substantially. Additionally, extended traveling also improves your productivity, problem-solving skills and can even increase your chances of getting promoted at work.
